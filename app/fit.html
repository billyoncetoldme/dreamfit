Go from Bra Woe to Bra Pro

With so many parts, styles and shapes at play, it’s no wonder so many of us get stuck with the “I guess this fits” bra… Get in the know to find your DreamFit, every time.

BACK CLOSURE
Describes the fastening of a bra’s wings. A back closure typically falls between the shoulder blades. A hook-and-eye closure is most common.
BACK SMOOTHING
A bra shapes front and back. Flexible back smoothing U-back bras create a 360-silhouette that’s just you. No ripple, pinch or bulge.
BACK WINGS
Bands of fabric running from cup to back closure. Weight and fabric type (knit or rigid) play a role in how much support the back wings lend to the bra.
CENTER GORE
The small pane of fabric between cups – usually a triangle or trapezoid shape. In nearly all bra styles, the center gore should lie flat against your sternum.
CONVERTIBLE
A bra that can be worn as a halter, x-cross, with one or both straps, or strapless. A must-have for single-shoulder looks and evening dresses.
PADDED
A bra with a gradual padding to the lower curve of the cup. A padded bra offers a little elevation and promotes a natural round, full breast shape. Padded pitches in!
PLUNGE
A bra with angled cups and a thin centered gore. A good plunge bra achieves a deep neckline without sacrficing coverage, no matter your size.
PUSH-UP
A bra that matches strategic padding with an angled cup, creating the illusion of greater cleavage.
SEAMLESS
You guessed it – a bra cup or fabric covering without a seam. A seamless bra shapes your silhouette without distracting from your look.
SUPPORT
X-cross: Back straps that cross between the shoulderblades for added support and better posture. Chest open, shoulders back... no nagging necessary!
STRAPS
Adjustable: With small plastic or metal slides, adjustable straps can be tightened or loosened. Don’t ignore these when trying on a bra for the first time! Strap length is a major part of finding the perfect fit.
Comfort
Wider straps covered in microfiber, comfort straps distribute the weight of the breast evenly. This means better support and lift, and none of the midday shoulder dig.
MICROFIBER
A silky, knit fabric made of superthin thread. Microfiber is soft and comfortable with a bit of stretch, making it the ideal choice for a garment that moves with you. Did somebody say seamless?
UNDERWIRE
Like the backbone of a bra’s cups, the underwire is a rigid support sewn into the cushion that helps maintain the curve of the bust. In a well-fitting bra, it rests flat against your body with no pinch or poke.
U-BACK
The classic U or leotard shape of the U-back bra provides better support and minimizes strap slip. Weight off your shoulders... straps on!
WIRE FREE
Easier to care for than bras that rely on underwire for support, wire free bras typically have softer cups and gentler, smoother silhouette lines.
A good bra should cost...
A) 4 main parts
B) 10 parts
C) 30 small parts
D) never really fit me
Answer: C! The average bra has more than 30 parts. Think they’re difficult to purchase? Try to make one!
The average bra has...
A) $30-$50
B) $60+
C) $9.98
D) $20-$30
Answer: C! Ah, the myth of the expensive bra. A good bra offers stylish support, every day. And it holds up over time. If you can do all that for $9.98, why wouldn’t you?
The best color bra to wear under a white tee is...
A) white, duh!
B) nude
C) grey
D) B or C
Answer: D! No matter your skin tone, white under white is actually more visible than nude or grey. Avoid matching the color of your bra to your top!
A bra’s support comes from...
A) the straps and band
B) the cups
C) wishful thinking
D) A&B
Answer: D! A full cup contributes almost as much as a bra strap in certain styles.
TRUE OR FALSE: A full-figured bra either looks great or has great coverage – never both.
Answer: FALSE. The styling of a bra enhances cleavage and silhouette, and a full cup keeps coverage “Superbowl Safe.” You can have it both ways!
An underwire is good for...
A) nothing
B) poking that same spot every time you twist
C) supporting breast tissue
D) maintaining the curve of a bra cup
Answer: C! The underwire actually contributes a great deal to the support of each cup, helping provide the structure that keeps weight off your shoulders.
The center gore of a bra should lie...
A) over the breasts, pressing cleavage together
B) flat against the breastbone
C) an inch below the underside of the breast
D) only to spare your feelings
Answer: B! When the center gore lies flat against the breastbone, you know your bra is giving support, not just coverage.
